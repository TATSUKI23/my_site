Dissertation

Steiner angle frame arrangement
- in-plate mechanical propaties and structural morphogenesis program with Grasshopper -
Overview
 In recent years, the academic field of biomimetics, which aims to create new things by mimicking the movements, functions, and forms of living organisms, has been attracting attention. Research is being conducted in a variety of fields, including medicine and machinery.

 One of the form that exists in nature is the minimal surface of the soap film. This is a property of soap film where the surface tension on the membrane is minimized, and adjacent soap film join together to minimize each other's surface tension, and the joining point is mathematically called the Steiner point. Any line connecting Steiner points form an angle of 120°.

 Various geometries such as hexagonal lattices have been studied recently for the frame arrangement. Therefore, I have studied a framework arrangement that forms this 120° angle (hereinafter referred to as "Steiner angle frame arrangement"), which has not been studied.
